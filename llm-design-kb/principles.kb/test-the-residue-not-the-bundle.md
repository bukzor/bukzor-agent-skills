# Test the Residue, Not the Bundle

When judging whether an artifact class deserves to exist, don't
evaluate the bundle it currently carries — most bundles contain
payloads that would survive anywhere. First give every payload to its
best rival home (decisions to the design tower, work items to tasks,
lessons to their distilled form, history to version control). Judge
the class solely on the unabsorbed residue: if what remains can still
name its reader and its read-back moment, the class has earned
existence; if not, the class was a container, not a kind.

The tell that this test was skipped: a class defended by its best
contents rather than by its unique ones. "The session log holds our
decisions" is a defense of decisions, not of session logs.
