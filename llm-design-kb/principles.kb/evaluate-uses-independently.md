# Evaluate Uses Independently

A single mechanism or notation can serve more than one purpose at
once — one path-bearing, one a load directive; one for humans, one
for agents. When deciding whether to keep, change, or retire it,
each distinct use gets its own verdict. Treating "should we keep X"
as one question, when X actually names two things bundled under one
name, produces a wrong answer for at least one of them — usually the
one that wasn't examined on its own terms.

Before rendering a verdict on any mechanism, enumerate its actual
uses first. If they have different coupling, cost, or risk profiles,
they need different decisions, stated separately, even if the
mechanism's name stays singular.
