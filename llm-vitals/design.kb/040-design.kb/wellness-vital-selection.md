# Wellness Vital Selection

The four recommended wellness vitals (Body, Relationships,
Restoration, Meaning) are a design choice with deliberation worth
recording. This entry captures the selection criteria, the
taxonomies surveyed, and the per-vital rationale so the selection
can be productively revisited.

## Selection criteria

A wellness vital must:

1. **Independently predict next-day cognitive performance.** Each
   vital should be a known driver of work-day functioning. Vitals
   that don't predict performance are noise.
2. **Rot independently of the others.** Vitals that co-vary tightly
   are redundant — they receive the same signal from one axis.
3. **Fit working memory when reviewed together.** ~4 is the upper
   bound of distinct concerns the operator can hold at a glance.
   Fewer underspecifies the surface; more dilutes attention.
4. **Cover non-task domains.** Vitals must capture texture that
   hour-counting can't — wellness is precisely the domain where
   "did you do the task" is the wrong question.

## Taxonomies surveyed

None of the academic taxonomies map cleanly to daily measurement.
They inform but don't dictate.

- **Self-Determination Theory** (Deci & Ryan): autonomy, competence,
  relatedness. Three needs; strongest empirical base in psychology.
- **Max-Neef's fundamental needs**: subsistence, protection,
  affection, understanding, participation, leisure, creation,
  identity, freedom. Nine, non-substitutable; standard in
  development economics.
- **PERMA** (Seligman): positive emotion, engagement, relationships,
  meaning, accomplishment. Five, flourishing-oriented.
- **Maslow**: physiological → safety → belonging → esteem →
  self-actualization. Cross-cultural evidence weak.
- **Nussbaum's central capabilities**: ten; philosophical / policy.
- **Lifestyle-medicine practitioner lists**: sleep, movement,
  nutrition, sunlight, social, stress management. Most actionable;
  weakest theoretical pedigree.

## The four

### Body

Sleep, movement, food, daylight. Threshold: each touched
≥N times/week.

Combines the four lifestyle-medicine subitems into one journal-kind
vital so the operator can capture texture across them in a single
entry (e.g., "slept 4h, moved well, ate poorly"). Predicts cognitive
performance most directly of any wellness vital.

### Relationships

Substantive contact with family and friends. Threshold:
≥M conversations/week, *not* text threads.

Loneliness is among the strongest predictors of cognitive and
physical decline. Distinct from professional contact (which lives in
`customer` etc.). Asynchronous text explicitly excluded — quantity
of pings is not quality.

### Restoration

Solitude, stillness, time outdoors, time without screens.
Threshold: ≥K hours/week.

The recovery substrate. Distinct from sleep (which is in Body) in
that restoration is *waking* recovery — the mode where the
default-mode network gets unstructured time. Without restoration,
processing depth collapses and decisions become reactive.

### Meaning / Play

Creative or purpose-aligned activity unrelated to work output.
Threshold: ≥J hours/week.

The "this matters to me even if nothing comes of it" domain. Both
creative play and meaning-making fit. The threshold is for time
spent in this mode, not output produced.

## Rejected

### Mind (deliberately absent)

A "mental" vital would double-count work tracks. Focused work,
learning, and tech exploration already live in `tech`, `exploration`,
and (for non-work learning) `meaning`. Adding `mind` as wellness
creates an axis overlapping three existing vitals — bad factoring.

### Lifestyle-medicine five (sleep / movement / nutrition / sunlight / social)

Too granular. Five vitals collapse into ~2 of ours (Body +
Relationships), then mix mechanism (sleep) with content (social).
Better to journal across them within one Body entry than fragment
into separate vitals.

### PERMA / PERMA-V

PERMA's "engagement" and "accomplishment" overlap heavily with work
tracks; PERMA-V's "vitality" maps to our Body. The framework is
flourishing-oriented (positive psychology); our system is
maintenance-oriented (don't let it rot). Different goal, different
factoring.

### Six or more vitals

Above ~4, working-memory parsing degrades. Fold sub-aspects into
journal-entry texture within fewer vitals rather than adding axes.
Re-examine if the operator reliably handles 4 and asks for more.

## When to revisit

- If two vitals consistently co-vary in journal entries — consider
  merging.
- If a vital is never the most-in-debt across a quarter — consider
  whether it's tracking something real.
- If the operator wants a fifth — ask whether it decomposes from an
  existing vital (split) or genuinely adds an independent axis (add).
