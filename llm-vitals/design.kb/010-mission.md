# Mission

Maintain minimum effort across the multiple life and work domains a
one-person operator must keep alive. Surface imbalance before it
compounds into dropped balls, lost revenue, or rotted wellness.

## Problem

A solo operator spans many irreducible domains — wellness, family,
revenue, customer obligations, ops, marketing, deep technical work —
with no team to delegate to. Each domain has a minimum required effort
below which it begins to rot. The operator has more recorded
intentions than working memory, naturally biases toward enjoyable
categories over necessary ones, and lacks a forcing function that
surfaces imbalance before crisis.

## Beneficiary

Solo founders, one-person enterprises, and individual contributors
whose lives and work span multiple irreducible domains. Explicitly
single-user; the pattern generalizes to small teams but the system
optimizes for one.

## Success

The operator can answer "which domain is most in debt?" without
reviewing dozens of separate lists. Imbalances surface within their
natural review cadence. Tech-bias reliably loses to revenue-debt when
the latter is visible. Wellness debt — usually invisible — becomes
visible early enough to act on.
