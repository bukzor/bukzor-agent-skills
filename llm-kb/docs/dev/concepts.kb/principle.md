# Concept: principle

A memorable, short aphorism that captures a methodological norm. Softer than a
procedure (no steps); heavier than a glossary entry (carries normative weight,
not just meaning).

## Shape

- The principle itself -- one memorable phrase, worth remembering verbatim.
- Brief expansion -- what it means, when it applies.
- Counter-pattern (optional) -- what the principle warns against.
- See-also (optional) -- procedures that embody it, failure modes it addresses.

## When to use principle (vs. procedure)

- The norm is too small to be a procedure (no ordered steps).
- The norm has memorable phrasing worth quoting ("container thinking is not
  content thinking").
- The norm cuts across many procedures.

## Filename

Kebab-case, descriptive of the principle. The filename should evoke the
aphorism it captures:

- `content-thinking-not-container-thinking.md`
- `smallest-relevant-scope-wins.md`
- `empty-parent-dirs-are-valid.md`

## Distinguished from

- A procedure -- has ordered steps; principles don't.
- A heuristic -- context-dependent decision rule; principles are more
  universal aphorisms.
- A glossary entry -- defines a word; principles assert a norm.
