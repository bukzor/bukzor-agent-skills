# Authorship Boundary

Every knowledge artifact declares its author class, and
machine-authored content never masquerades as operator judgment.
Claude-curated observations (auto-memory, distillations, summaries)
are labeled and size-bounded; operator-judgment content (wellness
entries, postmortem scores, contested positions) is authored only by
the operator, with Claude limited to surfacing prompts.

Generalizes the v1 vitals automation boundary: where the honest human
assessment *is* the data, automation poisons it at the source.
Checkable: no Claude-authored text in operator-judgment files;
knowledge that later turns out confabulated is traceable to its
author class.
