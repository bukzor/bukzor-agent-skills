# Write Economy

The companion to token-economy, one step earlier: token-economy
prices the load; write-economy prices the artifact's existence.
Every durable artifact must repay its write-and-carry cost with
reads. "Is this worth capturing?" is the wrong question — the right
one is "when is this read back, and by whom?" No answer, no write.

V1's evidence: devlogs written every session and read approximately
never; migration narratives kept after their sweep landed; indexes
restating what `ls` already says. Each felt responsible to write;
each was landfill — and worse than nothing, because a store that
accumulates unread writes teaches readers to stop trusting reads.

Carry cost exceeds tokens: every durable artifact is also a rename
surface, a validation surface, and a staleness liability.
